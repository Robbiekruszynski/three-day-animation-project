Griffon Vulture

The griffon vulture is a large Old World vulture in the bird of prey family Accipitridae. It is also known as the Eurasian griffon. It is not to be confused with a different species, Rüppell's griffon vulture. It is closely related to the white-backed vulture. -Altitude - 37,000ft -Wingspan: 7.5 – 9.2 ft. -Family: Accipitridae -Conservation status: Least Concern (Population increasing) Encyclopedia of Life -Scientific name: Gyps fulvus -Mass: Female: 14 – 25 lbs, Male: 14 – 23 lbs

Mallard

The mallard is a dabbling duck that breeds throughout the temperate and subtropical Americas, Eurasia, and North Africa and has been introduced to New Zealand, Australia, Peru, Brazil, Uruguay, Argentina, Chile, Colombia, the Falkland Islands, and South Africa.

Lifespan: 5 – 10 years (In the wild)

Scientific name: Anas platyrhynchos

Mass: 1.6 – 3.5 lbs (Adult)

Clutch size: Female: 8 – 13 (Adult)

Wingspan: 2.7 – 3.2 ft. (Adult)

Did you know: Almost all varieties of domestic duck apart from the Muscovy duck (Cairina moschata) are descended from the mallard (Anas platyrhynchos).

Stork

Storks are large, long-legged, long-necked wading birds with long, stout bills. They belong to the family called Ciconiidae, and make up the order Ciconiiformes. Ciconiiformes previously included a number of other families, such as herons and ibises, but those families have been moved to other orders.

Family: Ciconiidae; J. E. Gray, 1840

Scientific name: Ciconiidae

Wingspan: Marabou stork: 7.4 – 9.4 ft.

Higher classification: Ciconiiformes

Mass: White stork: 7.4 lbs, Black stork: 6.5 lbs, MORE Encyclopedia of Life

Did you know: Storks were once thought to be monogamous, but this is only partially true.

Falcon

Falcons are birds of prey in the genus Falco, which includes about 40 species. Falcons are widely distributed on all continents of the world except Antarctica, though closely related raptors did occur there in the Eocene.

Speed: Peregrine falcon: 240 mph

Scientific name: Falco

Family: Falconidae

Wingspan: Peregrine falcon: 2.4 – 3.9 ft.

Mass: Peregrine falcon: 1.5 – 3.3 lbs, Gyrfalcon: 3.2 lbs Encyclopedia of Life

Did you know: The traditional term for a male falcon is tercel (British spelling) or tiercel (American spelling), from Latin tertius = third because of the belief that only one in three eggs hatched a male bird. beautyofbirds.com

Geese

Geese are waterfowl of the family Anatidae. This group comprises the genera Anser and Branta. Chen, a genus comprising 'white geese', is sometimes used to refer to a group of species that are more commonly placed within Anser. Some other birds, mostly related to the shelducks, have "goose" as part of their names.

Lifespan: Canada goose: 10 – 24 years

Mass: Canada goose: 7.1 – 14 lbs

Scientific name: Anserini

Higher classification: Anserinae

Clutch size: Canada goose: 2 – 9, Swan goose: 5 – 6

Did you know: The average incubation period for a goose is approximately 28-34 days.

Blue Jay

The blue jay is a passerine bird in the family Corvidae, native to North America. It resides through most of eastern and central United States, although western populations may be migratory. Resident populations are also found in Newfoundland, Canada, while breeding populations can be found in southern Canada.

Scientific name: Cyanocitta cristata

Mass: 2.3 – 3.8 oz Encyclopedia of Life

Conservation status: Least Concern (Population stable)
